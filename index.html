Introduction

Global warming, driven by human activities, is one of the most significant environmental challenges...

Global warming, driven by human activities, is one of the most significant environmental challenges facing our planet today. Here are key aspects to understand:

  • Temperature Rise: The Earth's average temperature has increased by approximately 1.1°C since pre-industrial times, with most of this warming occurring in the past 40 years.
  • Greenhouse Effect: Carbon dioxide levels have risen from 280 parts per million to over 420 parts per million in the last 150 years, intensifying the greenhouse effect.
  • Ocean Impact: Over 90% of excess heat is absorbed by oceans, leading to marine ecosystem disruption and sea level rise of 3.3mm per year.
  • Extreme Weather: Global warming has led to a 30% increase in extreme weather events since 1990, including more frequent heatwaves, storms, and droughts.
  • Arctic Changes: The Arctic is warming twice as fast as the global average, with summer sea ice declining by 13% per decade.

The consequences of global warming extend beyond environmental impacts, affecting agriculture, human health, economies, and biodiversity worldwide. The Paris Agreement aims to limit global temperature rise to well below 2°C, preferably to 1.5°C, compared to pre-industrial levels.

Recent studies indicate that immediate action in the next decade is crucial. Without significant reduction in greenhouse gas emissions, global temperatures could rise by 4°C by 2100, leading to catastrophic environmental changes.

Causes of Global Warming

The primary drivers of global warming stem from human activities that have intensified over the past century. Here are the key causes:

Industrial Emissions

Industrial processes account for 32% of global emissions:

  • Manufacturing and production: 24% of total emissions
  • Chemical processing: 5% of global greenhouse gases
  • Mining operations: 3% of carbon footprint

Transportation

Transportation contributes 28% of greenhouse gas emissions:

  • Road vehicles: 72% of transport emissions
  • Aviation: 11% of transport emissions
  • Shipping: 14% of transport emissions

Deforestation

Forest loss accounts for 15% of emissions:

  • Tropical deforestation: 8 million hectares annually
  • Carbon storage loss: 1.5 billion tons of carbon yearly
  • Reduced carbon absorption capacity: 25% decline since 1990

Waste Management

Waste handling contributes 3% of emissions:

  • Landfills: 2% of total emissions
  • Wastewater treatment: 1.3% of emissions
  • Incineration: 0.5% of waste sector emissions

These human activities have increased atmospheric CO2 from 280 parts per million to over 420 parts per million since the Industrial Revolution, leading to enhanced greenhouse effects and global temperature rise.

Global Recycling Trends

The graph shows a steady increase in global recycling rates from 32% in 2019 to 45% in 2023. Plastic recycling has also improved from 20% to 30% during this period, indicating growing awareness and better recycling infrastructure.

Organic waste makes up 44% of global waste, followed by paper (17%), plastic (12%), glass (5%), metal (4%), and other materials (18%). Understanding this composition helps in developing targeted recycling strategies.
